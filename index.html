About

I am a PhD Candidate in Economics at the University of Texas at Dallas. My work studies macroeconomic fluctuations, monetary dynamics, and the historical evolution of economic volatility and uncertainty.

I am especially interested in using time series methods to place recent macroeconomic shocks in a longer historical context.

Research Interests

  • Macroeconomics
  • Monetary Economics
  • Historical Macroeconomic Volatility and Uncertainty
  • Time Series Analysis

Working Papers

A Century of Inflation and Economic Uncertainty: Putting the COVID-19 Shock in Context

Working paper

This project examines inflation and economic uncertainty across a long historical horizon, placing the COVID-19 shock in context alongside earlier episodes of macroeconomic volatility. The paper uses time series analysis to compare recent dynamics with historical patterns in inflation, uncertainty, and broader economic fluctuations.
